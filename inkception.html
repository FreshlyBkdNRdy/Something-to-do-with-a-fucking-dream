FLOW TYPES ──────────────────────── • Straight Baseline – rigid, minimal, clean. • Soft Arch – gentle rise, sentimental tone. • Deep Arch – dramatic emphasis arc. • S-Curve – flowing, organic, universally forgiving. • Wave Flow – rhythmic elevation and drop. • Diagonal Rise – assertive upward energy. • Diagonal Fall – somber downward tone. • Radiant Burst – center radiating outward. COMPOSITION TEMPLATES ──────────────────────── • Golden Spiral – elegant curve for emotional phrases. • S-Curve – default for script harmony. • Pyramid Stack – strong central emphasis. • Rule of Thirds – balanced, photographic logic. • Double Diagonal – tension and dynamic direction. • Radiating Center – symbolic, devotional layouts. • Vertical Flow – ribs, sternum, spine placement. • Horizontal Flow – forearms, collarbone. SCRIPT STYLES ──────────────────────── • Chicano Script – flourished capitals, emotional weight. • Minimal Script – thin, modern, breathable. • Gothic / Blackletter – authoritative, sharp geometry. • Calligraphy – graceful ink-stroke emphasis. • Old English – historic weight, ornamental serifs. • Clean Sans Script – quiet, modern lettering. BODY ZONES ──────────────────────── • Forearm – horizontal flow, medium contrast. • Bicep – flexible, neutral curvature. • Chest – wide arcs, requires added spacing. • Ribs – vertical flow, pain-zone spacing boost. • Sternum – narrow vertical paths, breathing space required. • Collarbone – curved baseline, gentle arcs. • Wrist – tight spacing, minimal flourishes. AGING / SPACING ──────────────────────── • Add 2–3% letter spacing for long-term clarity. • Add 3% flourish spacing to prevent merging. • Avoid curl-overlap on long swashes. • Increase negative space for ribs/chest/sternum. • Favor simpler capitals for small zones. • Preserve readability over ornament. ARTIST TIPS ──────────────────────── • Always prioritize readability over decoration. • Match composition to body flow, not the other way around. • Respect the client’s natural posture. • Avoid over-stacking lines unless intentional. • Add breathing room around emotional words. • Let flourishes “escape” outward, not inward.
